Through the Generations

Red, Blue, & Green

Generation 1
The original titles that introduced players to the world of Pokémon, featuring 151 creatures to catch and battle.

Gold & Silver

Generation 2
Introduced 100 new Pokémon, day-night cycles, and the Johto region, expanding the Pokémon universe.

Ruby & Sapphire

Generation 3
Set in the Hoenn region, these games introduced double battles and Pokémon abilities.

Diamond & Pearl

Generation 4
Introduced the Sinnoh region, 107 new Pokémon, and online trading and battling via the Nintendo Wi-Fi Connection.

Black & White

Generation 5
Set in the Unova region, these games introduced 156 new Pokémon acting as a new reset to the universe as you only able to catch the new 156, also included and a more narrative-driven experience.

X & Y

Generation 6
Set in the Kalos region, these games introduced 3D graphics as the first games on the 3ds, Created Mega Evolutions, and trainer customization.

Sun and Moon

Generation 7
Set in the Alola region, these games replaced traditional gyms with island trials and introduced z-moves and regional variants of existing Pokémon as a new way to bring older Pokemon back into the spotlight.

Sword & Shield

Generation 8
Set in the Galar region, these games introduced Dynamaxing and the Wild Area, an open-world section with dynamic weather and Pokémon encounters.

Scarlet & Violet

Generation 9
Set in the Paldea region, these titles offer an open-world experience with three distinct storylines and the introduction of the Terastal phenomenon.
